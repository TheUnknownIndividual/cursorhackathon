Presidential Order on Energy Sector Reforms
Order №1209 · 29 May 2019
Accelerating energy-sector reforms
  • Legal basis for liberalising power sector
  • Increasing competition in energy markets
  • Transition to green energy & efficiency

This Presidential Order set out a roadmap for liberalising Azerbaijan's electricity market, introducing competitive pricing, and preparing the legal ground for major renewable energy investments over the following decade.

  • Mandated unbundling of generation, transmission, and distribution
  • Directed tariff reform towards cost-reflective pricing
  • Paved way for independent power producers (IPPs)
  • Referenced in subsequent RES Law and Energy Law
Implemented

Renewable Energy Sources Law
Law №339-VIQ · 31 May 2021
Framework law for renewables
  • Defines RES categories and legal/economic rules
  • Long-term offtake contracts (PPAs)
  • Priority grid-connection for renewables

The cornerstone legislation for Azerbaijan's renewable energy sector. It defines what qualifies as a renewable source, establishes the rights and duties of producers and grid operators, and sets out the auction mechanism for new capacity.

  • Guarantees state offtake contracts for up to 20 years
  • Establishes net-metering for consumers up to 150 kW
  • Requires grid operators to accept RES electricity
  • Basis for all subsequent auctions by AREA

Law on Energy Efficiency
Law №359-VIQ · July 2021
Efficient use of energy resources
  • Mandatory energy audits for large consumers
  • Energy efficiency fund established
  • ESCO contracts & incentives

Passed alongside the RES Law in 2021, this legislation complements Azerbaijan's renewable push by reducing overall demand, making the transition more achievable and cost-effective.

  • Enterprises >1,000 toe/year must conduct energy audits
  • Building energy performance certification introduced
  • State fund provides low-interest loans for efficiency measures
  • Energy managers required for large public facilities

Public-Private Partnership Law
Law · December 2022
State-private collaboration
  • Up to 30-year contract terms
  • Investment stability guarantees
  • Attracts major international developers

The PPP Law provides the legal foundation for large-scale renewable energy projects developed in partnership between the state and private investors, ensuring long-term revenue certainty.

  • Government guarantees on contractual obligations
  • Dispute resolution via international arbitration
  • Used by Masdar, ACWA Power, and other IPPs
  • Supports BOOT and BOT models for power plants
Active

Law on Energy (Energetika haqqında)
Law №1006-VIQ · Oct 2023
New fundamental energy law
  • Replaces 1998 Energy Law
  • Market-based energy activities
  • Protecting consumer rights

A landmark update that replaced the outdated 1998 Law on Energy, bringing Azerbaijan's primary energy legislation in line with modern market principles and EU energy law concepts.

  • Defines roles of all energy sector participants
  • Introduces concept of "active consumer" (prosumer)
  • Establishes independent regulation principles
  • Framework for energy storage and smart grids
Active

Law on Electric Power

Modern power sector regulation
  • Unbundling of generation, transmission, distribution
  • Active consumer (prosumer) rules
  • Market liberalisation roadmap

This law governs the production, transmission, distribution, and sale of electricity, establishing clear rules for all participants in the electricity value chain.

  • Regulates grid access rights for renewable producers
  • Net-metering rules codified at legislative level
  • Protects consumers from discriminatory pricing
  • Cabinet Decree No. 346 (Sept 2023) implements net-metering provisions

Law on Environmental Impact Assessment
Law №1175-VQ · June 2018
Environmental protection
  • Mandatory EIA for all energy projects
  • Strategic environmental assessment
  • Public participation in decisions

Requires thorough environmental impact assessment before renewable energy projects can receive permits, ensuring ecological considerations are integrated into energy planning.

Tax Incentives & Investment Benefits
Tax Code · Updated 2025
Up to 30-year tax holidays
  • Full exemption from corporate tax (30 yrs)
  • 100% property & land tax exemption
  • VAT & customs duty waived on equipment

Azerbaijan offers one of the most competitive fiscal packages for renewable energy investors in the region, applying across generation, grid connection, and residential installations.

  • Residential solar up to 150 kW: fully tax-exempt
  • Profit tax holiday for 7–10 years for small projects
  • Zero import duty on turbines, panels, inverters
  • Simplified licensing: single-window through AREA
Active

PPA & Auction Framework
RES Law · 2021
Guaranteed offtake contracts
  • Long-term PPAs with state companies
  • Competitive tender mechanism
  • Fixed tariff guarantees & revenue certainty

The auction framework managed by AREA runs competitive tenders for new renewable capacity, with winners signing long-term Power Purchase Agreements with Azərenerji.

  • Tariffs set competitively; recent solar: ~$0.02/kWh
  • Denominated in USD with guaranteed conversion
  • State guarantees payment obligations
  • Force-majeure clauses protect investors

Administrative Authority for Energy Violations
Order №F-95 · Ministry of Energy · 2019
Administrative procedures and enforcement
  • List of authorized officials for violations
  • Updates to Order F-108 (Dec 2, 2019)

This ministerial order defines which officials within the Ministry of Energy are authorised to handle administrative proceedings for violations of energy legislation.

  • Authorised officers can issue fines and compliance orders
  • Supersedes earlier fragmented enforcement structure

Support Schemes & Guarantees
RES Law Framework
State support mechanisms
  • Feed-in tariff system for small producers
  • Guaranteed long-term PPAs
  • Priority grid access for renewables

A suite of financial and administrative support measures ensures that both small residential producers and large independent power producers can operate viably.

  • Net-metering: surplus exported to grid at retail price
  • Feed-in tariff: fixed for 20 years for <1 MW plants
  • Priority dispatch: RES electricity dispatched before fossil fuel
  • State investment guarantees covering political risk
